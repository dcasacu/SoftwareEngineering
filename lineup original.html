The problem

People love local markets. They just hate the wait.

Traditional markets are full of character and quality — but long queues, paper tickets and disorientation push shoppers toward supermarkets and delivery apps.

⏳

Wasted time in line

Customers can spend 15–30 minutes waiting at a single stall — and skip the market altogether when they're in a rush.

🗺️

Hard to navigate

New visitors don't know which shops are open, where to find them, or what they sell. Signage rarely helps.

📝

Paper tickets, lost turns

Owners juggle ticket dispensers and shouts, with no real visibility into peak hours or daily performance.

The solution

One app for the whole market.

LineUp turns every stall into a smart, connected counter. Customers pick a market, find the shops they need, and join queues from anywhere — even before they leave home.

  • ✓
    Real-time queues — Always know your position and estimated wait.
  • ✓
    Live shop info — Open/closed status, categories and average service time.
  • ✓
    Zero friction — No login required. Tap and you're in line.
  • ✓
    Owner-friendly — A simple dashboard, not another point-of-sale headache.

Innovation

What makes LineUp different?

LineUp is not just another queue app. It is a solution designed specifically for local markets, connecting customers, stalls and queue management in one interactive experience.

✦ Specialized in local markets

Most queue systems are designed for restaurants, hospitals or generic businesses. LineUp focuses on traditional markets, where many small stalls, different queues and customer movement happen at the same time.

✦ Interactive map, not just information

The market map is not only informative. Customers can discover stalls, check their status and join a queue directly from the map, turning navigation into action.

✦ Value for customers and owners

Customers save time and avoid waiting physically in line, while owners manage their queue more easily and keep better control of their daily workflow.

✦ Daily queue statistics for owners

At the end of the working day, shop owners can review useful stats such as customers served, busiest hours and queue performance, helping them understand demand and improve service.
